Adstock Theory

The adstock transformation is a fundamental concept in Marketing Mix Modeling that captures how marketing effects persist over time.

Mathematical Definition

The geometric adstock model is defined as:

\[\text{Adstock}_t = \text{Media}_t + \alpha \cdot \text{Adstock}_{t-1}\]

Where: - \(\text{Adstock}_t\) is the adstocked value at time \(t\) - \(\text{Media}_t\) is the raw media spend at time \(t\) - \(\alpha\) is the decay parameter (0 < α < 1) - \(\text{Adstock}_{t-1}\) is the adstocked value from the previous period

Interpretation

The adstock parameter \(\alpha\) controls the persistence of marketing effects:

  • High α (close to 1): Marketing effects persist for many periods

  • Low α (close to 0): Marketing effects decay quickly

For example, with \(\alpha = 0.8\): - 80% of the previous period’s effect carries over - 20% of the effect decays each period

Half-Life Calculation

The half-life of adstock effects can be calculated as:

\[\text{Half-life} = \frac{\ln(0.5)}{\ln(\alpha)}\]

This tells us how many periods it takes for half of the marketing effect to decay.

Example

Consider a media campaign with the following spend pattern:

media_spend = [100, 0, 50, 0, 75]
alpha = 0.6

The adstocked values would be:

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} \text{Adstock}_1 &= 100 + 0.6 \cdot 0 = 100 \\ \text{Adstock}_2 &= 0 + 0.6 \cdot 100 = 60 \\ \text{Adstock}_3 &= 50 + 0.6 \cdot 60 = 86 \\ \text{Adstock}_4 &= 0 + 0.6 \cdot 86 = 51.6 \\ \text{Adstock}_5 &= 75 + 0.6 \cdot 51.6 = 105.96 \end{align}\end{split}\]

Implementation

In our implementation, the adstock transformation is applied using the geometric_adstock() function:

from src.utils import geometric_adstock

# Apply adstock transformation
adstocked_media = geometric_adstock(media_data, alpha=0.6)
